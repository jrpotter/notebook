## Overview

The **hyperbolic functions** are analogues of the [[circular|trigonometric]] functions but defined using the hyperbola instead of the circle.

%%ANKI
Cloze
The {1:hyperbolic} functions are to the {2:hyperbola} as the {2:circular} functions are to the {1:circle}.
Reference: Tom M. Apostol, _Calculus, Vol. 1: One-Variable Calculus, with an Introduction to Linear Algebra_, 2nd ed. (New York: Wiley, 1980).
<!--ID: 1769376091620-->
END%%

## Functions

### Hyperbolic Sine

The **hyperbolic sine** function, denoted $\sinh$, is defined as: $$\large \sinh{x} = \frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{2}$$

## Identities

There exist a number of identities analogous to those of the trigonemetric functions.

### Symmetries

The hyperbolic sine and hyperbolic tangent functions are [[algebra/functions#Symmetries|odd]] whereas the hyperbolic cosine function is [[algebra/functions#Symmetries|even]].

%%ANKI
Basic
Which of $\sinh$, $\cosh$, and $\tanh$ are odd functions?
Back: $\sinh$ and $\tanh$.
Reference: Tom M. Apostol, _Calculus, Vol. 1: One-Variable Calculus, with an Introduction to Linear Algebra_, 2nd ed. (New York: Wiley, 1980).
<!--ID: 1769376091669-->
END%%

%%ANKI
Basic
Which of $\sinh$, $\cosh$, and $\tanh$ are even functions?
Back: Just $\cosh$.
Reference: Tom M. Apostol, _Calculus, Vol. 1: One-Variable Calculus, with an Introduction to Linear Algebra_, 2nd ed. (New York: Wiley, 1980).
<!--ID: 1769376091672-->
END%%

### Pythagorean Identity

For any real number $t$, $\cosh^2(t) - \sinh^2(t) = 1$.

%%ANKI
Basic
What does the hyperbolic Pythagorean identity state?
Back: $\cosh^2{x} - \sinh^2{x} = 1$
Reference: Tom M. Apostol, _Calculus, Vol. 1: One-Variable Calculus, with an Introduction to Linear Algebra_, 2nd ed. (New York: Wiley, 1980).
<!--ID: 1769376091659-->
