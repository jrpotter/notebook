## Overview

Notes are based on D&D 5.5e.

## D20 Tests

A **D20 Test** is a d20 roll used to determine success or failure. The test follows these steps:

1. Roll 1d20.
2. Add Modifiers. These include the relevant [[creatures#Abilities|ability modifier]], proficiency bonus, and circumstantial bonuses/penalties.
3. Compare the total to a target number.

if a creature has **Advantage**, they instead roll 2d20 and choose the higher number. If a creature has **Disadvantage**, they instead roll 2d20 and choose the lower number. If a creature is in a situation that imposes both Advantage and Disadvantage, they only roll 1d20.

### Proficiency

All creatures have a **Proficiency Bonus** reflecting the impact that training has on a creature's capabilities. A character's Proficiency Bonus increases as they gain levels whereas a monster's Proficiency Bonus depends on their Challenge Rating.

Such a bonus is applied to a D20 Test when the creature has proficiency in a [[creatures#Skills|skill]], in a [[creatures#Saving Throws|saving throw]], or with an item. It is also used for spell attacks and for calculating the DC of saving throws for spells.

| Level or CR | Bonus |
| ----------- | ----- |
| Up to 4     | +2    |
| 5-8         | +3    |
| 9-12        | +4    |
| 13-16       | +5    |
| 17-20       | +6    |
| 21-24       | +7    |
| 25-28       | +8    |
| 29-30       | +9    |

### Difficulty Class

The **Difficulty Class** (DC) of an [[creatures#Ability Checks|ability check]] or [[creatures#Saving Throws|saving throw]] represents the tasks' difficulty. The more difficult the task, the higher its DC.

| Task Difficulty   | DC  |
| ----------------- | --- |
| Very easy         | 5   |
| Easy              | 10  |
| Medium            | 15  |
| Hard              | 20  |
| Very Hard         | 25  |
| Nearly Impossible | 30  |

Ability checks and saving throws use DC as their target number.
