## Vision

An area is said to be **Lightly Obscured** if vision is slightly obstructed. For example, in areas with patchy fog or moderate foliage. Wisdom (Perception) checks relying on sight are rolled with [[dnd/index#D20 Tests|Disadvantage]].

An area is said to be **Heavily Obscured** if vision is completely obscured. For example, in areas with heavy fog or dense foliage. Unless otherwise stated, creatures in Heavily Obscured areas are afflicted with the Blinded condition.

The presence or absence of light determines the category of illumination in an area. An area of **Bright Light** lets most creatures see normally. **Dim Light** creates a Lightly Obscured area. **Darkness** creates a Heavily Obscured area.

## Travel

While traveling outside [[combat]], a group can move at a **Fast**, **Normal**, or **Slow** pace. The relevant distances are covered in the following table:

| Page   | Minute   | Hour    | Day      |
| ------ | -------- | ------- | -------- |
| Fast   | 400 feet | 4 miles | 30 miles |
| Normal | 300 feet | 3 miles | 24 miles |
| Slow   | 200 feet | 2 miles | 18 miles |

Traveling on mounts doubles the speed of the group for an hour. After an hour, mounts need to make a Short or Long Rest before they can move at an increased pace again.

## Hazards

A **hazard** refers to any environmental danger.

* **Falling**.
	* A creature that falls takes 1d6 Bludgeoning damage at the end of the fall for every 10 feet it fell, to a maximum of 20d6. When the creature lands, it has the [[dnd/conditions#Prone|Prone]] condition unless it avoids taking any damage from the fall.
	* If falling into water or another liquid, the creature can use its Reaction to make a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check to hit the surface head or feet first. On success, any damage is halved.
