## Overview

Combat is organized into a cycle of rounds and turns. A round represents about 6 seconds in the game world. Within a round, a creature can perform movement up to their Speed, an [[#Actions|action]], a Bonus Action, and a Reaction.

## Initiative

The order of turns is determined at the beginning of combat when everyone rolls **Initiative**. This is a [[creatures#Ability Check|Dexterity check]] that determines a creature's turn in the Initiative order. Groups of identical creatures usually share Initiative.

If a combatant is **surprised** by combat starting, that combatant has Disadvantage on their Initiative roll.

If there is a tie in the Initiative checks:

* The DM decides the order among tied monsters.
* The DM decides the order among tied monsters and players.
* The players decide the order among tied players.

## Actions

An **action** refers to something other than moving or communicating. The main actions are sumarized as follows:

* **Attack**. Attack with a weapon or an Unarmed Strike.
* **Dash**. For the rest of the turn, give yourself extra movement equal to your Speed.
* **Disengage**. Your movement doesn't provoke Opportunity Attacks for the rest of the turn.
* **Dodge**. Until the start of your turn, attack rolls against you have Disadvantage, and you make Dexterity saving throws with Advantage.
	* You lose this benefit if you have the Incapacitated condition of if your Speed is 0.
* **Help**. Help another creature's ability check or attack roll, or administer first aid.
* **Hide**. Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check.
* **Influence**. Make a Charisma (Deception, Intimidation, Performance, or Persuasion), or Wisdom (Animal Handling) check to alter a creature's attitude.
* **Magic**. Cast a spell, use a magic item, or use a magical feature.
* **Ready**. Prepare to take an action in response to a trigger you define.
* **Search**. Make a Wisdom (Insight, Medicine, Perception, or Survival) check.
* **Study**. Make an Intelligence (Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, or Religion) check.
* **Utilize**. Use a nonmagical object.

## Movement

A creature can move up to a distance equal to their **Speed** on their turn. If moving on **Difficult Terrain**, every foot of movement costs 1 extra foot.

A creature may have special speeds such as Climb Speed, Fly Speed, or Swim Speed. If a creature has more than one speed, they can choose which one to use during their movement. They can switch between the speeds during their turn.

### Swimming

While swimming, each foot of movement costs 1 extra foot (2 extra feet in Difficult Terrain). This extra cost is ignored if the creature has a Swim Speed and uses it to swim.

When making a [[#Melee Attacks|melee attack roll]] with a weapon underwater, a creature that lacks a Swim Speed has Disadvantage on the attack roll unless the weapon deals Piercing damage. A [[#Ranged Attacks|ranged attack roll]] with a weapon underwater automatically misses a target beyond the weapon's normal range, and the attack roll has Disadvantage against a target within normal range.

Anything underwater has [[#Resistance|Resistance]] to Fire damage.

### Jumping

When jumping, a creature makes either a Long Jump or High Jump:

* *Long Jump*.
	* A creature leaps horizontally a number of feet up to its Strength score if it moves at least 10 feet immediately before the jump.
	* A standing Long Jump can only move half the distance.
	* If landing in Difficult Terrain, the creature must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or have the Prone condition.
* *High Jump*.
	* A creature leaps into the air a number of feet equal to 3 plus its Strength modifier (minimum of 0 feet) if it moves at least 10 feet immediately before the jump.
	* A standing High Jump can only move half the distance.

### Mounts

A willing creature at least one size larger than a rider and with an appropriate anatomy can serve as a mount. A creature can mount another if the two are within 5 feet of one another. Mounting or dismounting costs an amount of movement equal to half the creature's Speed (round down).

Controlled mounts take on the initiative of the rider. The mount moves on the rider's turn and may perform one of three [[#Actions|actions]]: Dash, Disengage, or Dodge. In contrast, an independent mount retains its place in the Initiative order and moves/acts as it likes.

If an effect moves a mount against its will, the rider must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity [[creatures#Saving Throws|saving throw]] or fall off, landing with the [[dnd/conditions#Prone|Prone]] condition in an unoccupied space within 5 feet of the mount. While mounted, the rider must make the same save if knocked Prone or the mount is knocked Prone.

## Attack Rolls

Attack rolls are [[dnd/index#D20 Tests|D20 Tests]] that determine whether an attack hits a target. An attack roll hits if the roll equals or exceeds the target's Armor Class. An ability modifier is added to the result of the attack roll depending on the type of attack:

* The Strength modifier is added if performing a melee attack with a weapon.
* The Strength modifier is added if performing an Unarmed Strike.
* The Dexterity modifier is added if performing a ranged attack with a weapon.
* The Spellcasting modifier is used for a spell attack.

### Melee Attacks

A **melee attack** refers to an attack that happens within reach. Unless otherwise noted, creatures have a 5-foot reach. A melee attack typically uses a hand-held weapon or an **Unarmed Strike**.

The latter refers to a melee attack using one's body to damage, grapple, or shove a target. A creature making an Unarmed Strike can choose one of the following three effects to take place:

* **Damage**. An attack roll is made against the target. The bonus equals the Strength modifier plus the [[dnd/index#Proficiency|Proficiency Bonus]].
	* On a hit, the target takes Bludgeoning damage equal to 1 plus the Strength modifier.
* **Grapple**. The target must succeed on a Strength or Dexterity saving throw (it chooses which). Otherwise it is [[dnd/conditions#Grappled|Grappled]].
	* The DC for the saving throw and any escape attempts equals 8 plus the Strength modifier and Proficiency Bonus.

### Ranged Attacks

A **ranged attack** refers to an attack that happens over a distance. A ranged weapon or spell can specify one or two ranges.

* Normal Range. If a single range is specified, the attacker cannot target something beyond this range.
* Long range. If two ranges are specified, attacks against targets further than the smaller range is made with Disadvantage. The attack cannot target something beyond the larger range.

A ranged attack is rolled with Disadvantage if made against a creature that can see the attacker, is within 5 feet of the attacker, and is not [[dnd/conditions#Incapacitated|Incapacitated]].

### Opportunity Attacks

## Damage

Each weapon, spell, and damaging monster ability specifies the damage it deals. When attacking with a weapon, one adds the same ability modifier used for the attack roll to the total damage. When attacking with a spell, the spell indicates which dice to roll and whether to add any modifiers.

If there's a penalty to the damage, it's possible to deal 0 damage but not negative damage. 

If the attack roll is a natural 20, the attack is considered a **Critical Hit**. The attack's damage dice is rolled twice. Relevant modifiers are added once as normal.

### Resistance

If a creature has **resistance** to a certain damage type, damage of that type is halved against them (round down). If a creature has **vulnerability** to a certain damage type, damage of that type is doubled against them.

1. Adjustments such as bonuses, penalites, or multipliers.
2. Resistance.
3. Vulnerability.

### Death Saving Throws

Whenever a creature starts their turn with 0 Hit Points, they must make a **Death Saving Throw**. The creature must make a 1d20 roll. If the roll is 10 or higher, they succeed. Otherwise, they fail. If a 1 is rolled, the creature suffers two failures. If a 20 is rolled, the creature instead regains 1 Hit Point.

The number of successes and failures reset to zero if the creature regains any Hit Points or becomes Stable. On the third success, a creature becomes Stable. On the third failure, the creature dies.

If any damage is received while at 0 Hit Points, the creature suffers a Death Saving Throw failure. If the damage is from a Critical Hit, the creature suffers two failures instead. If the damage meets or exceeds the creature's Hit Point maximum, they die.
